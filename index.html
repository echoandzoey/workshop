How it works

You'll be presented with a series of short sketches about different people, each facing a unique life situation. These stories are based on real-life experiences and aim to show the diversity of life paths and the invisible forces, such as social norms, networks, and systemic limitations, that shape financial decisions.

For each person, ask yourself:

What do I need?

You will have to choose between three options

No financial aid needed

You feel no need to use any form of financial or government support.

Long-term loan plan

A flexible loan, similar to borrowing from a bank, with low interest and a long repayment period (15â€“35 years). You decide how to use the money.

Personalized financial aid

This can range from light-touch advice to full financial oversight. It could be a targeted subsidy or involve a financial officer managing your finances, especially if you're struggling to manage them on your own.

After making your choice, you'll be prompted to reflect on why you made it.

Why this matters

This tool is not about right or wrong choices. It's about empathy, awareness, and reflection.

By walking through different perspectives, we hope this experience sparks deeper understanding, meaningful conversations, and maybe even a shift in how we view financial aid.
